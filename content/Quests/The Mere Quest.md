## The Mere Quest

**The tavern stinks of stale ale, damp wood, and too many unwashed bodies pressed together. The air is thick with laughter and murmured deals, but one voice cuts through it all—sharp, unimpressed, and unmistakably bored.**

_"So you all made it back. Huh, I guess I aught to cancel the rescue team"_ [[Yehnora]] leans lazily against the bar, wiping out a tankard with an old rag that might be making it dirtier. She eyes the party, lips curled into a smirk, like she’s already taken the measure of them and found them lacking.

_"Wait, you don't actually believe that I sent a rescue team right? You're all not that dumb."_ She says with a grin as she winks at the group before noticing the torn piece of paper as you all put it on the counter. _"So you picked [[Mere]]?"_

She sets the tankard down with a dull thud and props her elbow on the counter, chin resting on her palm. _"Listen, I don't know what stroke of dumb luck got you this far, but if you’re hoping to find someone in Mere and come back breathing, you’re either **very** brave or **very** stupid. My gold’s on the latter."_

She glances around the tavern, lowering her voice—but not by much. _"The guy you're looking for? Bad idea. People don’t just ‘go missing’ in Mere. They get **taken, buried, or regrettably digested**—in that order. And if he’s been gone more than a day, chances are, he’s already part of the scenery."_

Her eyes flicker over the group again, gauging their reaction. With a theatrical sigh, she straightens, finally pushing the crumpled scrap of parchment toward them.

_"Fine. Since you're so eager to get yourselves killed, here’s where he was last seen—a run-down shack near the river, not far from the old sawmill. If you find him, I’ll be shocked. If you find him **alive**, I might actually start believing in miracles."_

She gives them an exaggerated grin before turning away to fill another mug, throwing one last remark over her shoulder.

_"Just try not to make a mess on the way out, yeah? I’d hate to have to clean up after another batch of overconfident corpses."_

## Part 1

With Pymis choosing to remain behind in Revellia, the rest of the group set out on their next quest—one that would lead them into the heart of Mere. Yehnora, the bartender who had initially pointed them toward this path, offered little information and even less enthusiasm. She scoffed at their willingness to take on yet another perilous task, warning them with a weary shake of her head before sending them on their way. The surrounding patrons weren’t much more helpful, save for one common remark: in Mere, they would stand out, no matter what they did. Their appearance, their demeanor—everything about them would mark them as outsiders.

Their journey took them through the borderlands of Skarn, where the autumn air clashed with the unseasonal humidity, creating an oppressive, sticky heat. The terrain shifted into a sickly green landscape, more dirt than grass, the remnants of past rainfall turning the ground into a damp, uneven mess. Rona, unused to the abrupt climate change and the rough ride, succumbed to exhaustion, falling into a restless sleep as the coach jostled along the road.

Their driver, Joshua, rapped on the carriage’s side, his voice breaking through the rhythmic creaking of the wheels. A goblin blocked the path. The group moved swiftly, dispatching it with little difficulty, only to face the rest of its pack. Two hobgoblins fell before they could mount a proper resistance, and with that, the road was clear. Satisfied, Joshua dropped them at the last stretch before Mere, taking his leave as Rona, still drowsy, leaned against Tarvo for support.

By the time they reached Mere’s gates, the late afternoon sun cast long shadows over the road. Bandits stood as the town’s makeshift guards, their presence exuding an air of authority tinged with lawlessness. As Tarvo squared up to one of them, the leader sneered, questioning his intelligence. But before things could escalate, Arispira, recalling the advice given to them earlier, adopted an intimidating stance. With sharp words and an unwavering gaze, she forced their way through, pressing for information the guard didn’t even have. Reluctantly, he let them pass.

The first sight within Mere was a grim one—cages filled with the barely living and the long-dead, bodies rotting under the open sky. Blood stained the wood and stone, and the stench was nearly unbearable. Rona pressed a hand over her mouth as they surveyed their surroundings. A homeless encampment sprawled to one side, while hastily constructed shacks stood in the distance, mere husks of homes. The only place that seemed truly alive was the tavern.

They pushed through the doors of The Broken Keg, where the air was thick with smoke, stale beer, and the weight of many unsavory figures. A tiefling woman—Veronica—worked behind the bar, her experienced eyes scanning them as they entered. Tarvo, ever theatrical, kicked the door open, drawing more attention than necessary. Nearly everyone inside bore some affiliation with the Black Rose Gang, their presence a silent warning.

The group approached the bar, ordering drinks save for Rona, who abstained. It was then they met Kerrick, a local with a vested interest in the happenings of Mere. Generous with his coin, he covered their tab, and as they spoke, Veronica leaned in, revealing the true purpose of their quest. They were to locate a man named Daveed—the former mayor’s assistant before Mere had fallen into its current state. His knowledge had been the only thing keeping the warring gangs in check. Now, he was missing.

Veronica, however, had another proposal. In addition to finding Daveed, she wanted them to kill Mikhail, the man currently holding power over the old town hall, offering double the gold as an incentive. Her gang loomed behind her, their silent backing evident in the way they shifted, waiting for the group's response. But the party was uninterested in playing executioner, and they declined the offer. As they turned away, they were approached by a man named Red Jack—someone who, notably, was not affiliated with the Black Rose Gang. He offered them a few words about Mikhail, hinting that they should return to him later. He also mentioned something troubling: the sawmill, the town’s third major area, was home to assassins.

Before they stepped back into the streets, Kerrick shared a bit about himself, his words casual but revealing. Meanwhile, Sariel, insulted by an offhand remark from Tarvo, stormed ahead, only to pause as they noticed something off about the town’s structures. Tarvo, following after, soon confirmed the strange sensation. The bandits on patrol communicated in an unusual manner—a brief, almost imperceptible glow passing between them. Not wanting to attract unnecessary attention, they chose to move on, observing rather than interfering.

Approaching the old town hall, Ari’s eyes lingered on a burned-out building to the right. It left an uneasy feeling in her gut, though she said nothing. Meanwhile, Tarvo attempted to extract directions from a nearby bandit, who responded with immediate aggression. Rather than backing down, Tarvo matched the hostility, escalating the tension. The altercation did not go unnoticed, as the exchange was silently relayed to the other guards nearby. Undeterred, the group pressed forward, arriving at what appeared to be an old courthouse rather than a simple town hall.

Inside, Mikhail sat upon a throne, his presence one of arrogant authority. Tarvo, embracing his usual brand of aggression, kicked the doors open, drawing immediate hostility. Mikhail’s guards moved in response, but Kerrick cut through the tension with a question: did Mikhail know of someone named Gwendolyn? The response was vague, deflective, but it gave Kerrick the opportunity he needed to slip into the study. There, he unearthed a redacted document, the name Gwendolyn barely legible beneath the thick strokes of censorship.

Meanwhile, Tarvo continued to press Mikhail about Daveed, his confrontational nature at odds with the careful diplomacy Ari and they attempted to maintain. Sensing the rising tension, Rona slipped outside, taking in the cool air as she steadied herself. Eventually, the group decided they had learned all they could for now and left the hall behind.

Back at the inn, Kerrick spared no expense, securing the most lavish room available. As the group settled in, Ari lingered downstairs, catching sight of something that she chose to keep to herself—for the moment. Fatigue finally caught up with them. One by one, they collapsed into their beds, Rona the first to succumb to sleep, the weight of Mere pressing down on them all as the night stretched on.

## Part 2

As the party awoke at the crack of morning to the sounds of rain and fog, Kerrick noticed a letter for himself. Without drawing attention, he quickly hid it from the group and read it in secret. Meanwhile, Rona, feeling much better after the previous day's exhaustion, stood by the window with Tarvo, watching the mist settle over the streets below. Across the room, Sariel moved to wake a still-sleeping Arispira.

Once everyone was ready, they made their way downstairs. Arispira mentioned seeing a suspicious red stain in front of the doors the night before, prompting Sariel to join her in inspecting it. Kerrick, however, chose a more direct approach, slipping the innkeeper a bribe to unlock the doors. When they swung open, the source of the stain was revealed—spoiled fruit and vegetables, their juices leaking onto the floorboards.

Tarvo, noticing that all of the produce was imported, pointed it out to the group. Rona, unfazed, asked the innkeeper where they could get the best breakfast. The innkeeper, either misunderstanding or being deliberately vague, directed them to the casino—though he had likely meant the brothel.

Stepping outside, Rona approached a nearby guard, intending to ask for directions. Arispira and Sariel quickly intercepted her, reminding her that, given her condition the day before, she hadn’t yet realized the necessity of "acting tough." Ignoring their warnings, Rona pressed on, only to receive a gruff and dismissive response from the guard: "Don't ask that anymore." Still, she managed to get directions to the casino district near the markets.

As they entered the market and approached the casino district, they found themselves in a massive open space dominated by a repurposed sawmill, which now served as a gang headquarters. While moving through the area, Rona noticed scattered papers on the floor, some of which appeared to be bounties. She picked one up, her eyes narrowing at a name—someone she might know.

At the entrance to the casino, Tarvo noticed a disturbing amount of blood pooled near the doorway. While he examined it, Arispira and Kerrick moved to join him, while Sariel and Rona were intercepted by a courtesan inviting them into the brothel. As they politely declined, they were approached by another woman—Vaneesha. She spoke to Sariel urgently but cut herself short upon noticing a nearby guard. Rona attempted a distraction, but it failed, forcing Sariel and Rona to follow Vaneesha inside. Once seated with drinks in hand, they asked what was troubling her.

Meanwhile, Tarvo, Arispira, and Kerrick turned their attention to the two guards standing in blood. Tarvo examined it closely and determined it was human. Concerned but calculating, Kerrick and Arispira debated their next move. Tarvo, impatient and vengeful, muttered about killing them. In an attempt to diffuse the tension, Arispira suddenly mentioned getting coffee. Tarvo agreed but was urged toward decaf, as the others feared what might happen if he drank the real thing.

As they entered the casino, all eyes turned toward them. A group of assassins, casually playing cards, watched their every move. Sensing immediate danger, the trio backed out and regrouped with Sariel and Rona. Vaneesha, another courtesan forced into the role, revealed that her husband had been taken—he had worked with Daveed before his disappearance. Forced into her current role in his absence, she pleaded with them to find him quickly, warning that people didn’t last long in Mere. From her urgency, they deduced he was being held somewhere involving a doctor.

Taking on her request, the group split up. Rona, still curious about Mere’s inner workings, questioned the barkeep about who issued the bounties and what kind of person Daveed had been. She also ordered coffee for the rest of the group. Tarvo, ever defiant, slipped in his own order and drank it before anyone could stop him.

The party then made their way back into Black Axe territory, heading toward the town hall in search of information. As they arrived, they learned that Mikhail had retired to his quarters. Looking for an alternative approach, Rona took it upon herself to flirt with one of the guards, hoping to loosen their lips. Meanwhile, Kerrick used his imperial status to threaten another, forcing him to move. Under their distraction, Arispira slipped inside, finding documents detailing the Black Axe’s dealings in organ trafficking—an imminent shipment was on the way.

Sariel and Arispira, disturbed by what they found, decided to investigate the docks. Rona, still working the guard, managed to glean that there was a hidden basement where "messed up things happened." As they spoke, another guard entered the town hall and disappeared downstairs.

The party split up—Sariel and Arispira made their way toward the docks while Kerrick, Rona, and Tarvo descended into the basement. A fight quickly broke out, forcing them to cut down several guards. Meanwhile, at the docks, Arispira and Sariel found no boats yet, but quickly realized what was happening—the guards weren’t taking organs out to sea. They were collecting them from the town hall.

Realizing the connection, they rushed back just as the guards were preparing to collect the latest shipment for transport out to sea. A larger battle erupted. Everyone fought—except Arispira, who remained focused on finding information about Yerrick, Vaneesha’s missing husband.

When the dust settled, most of their enemies lay dead. Only one remained, spared by Sariel for interrogation. As the party caught their breath, they knew they had only just begun unraveling Mere’s darkest secrets.
