#### **The Cost of Reversal**

_"Life and death are not solitary events; they are threads in the grand weave of the world. To sever and reattach one is to disturb the entire pattern. The world resists this, shifting to correct what should not have changed."_

_"The old dragons knew this truth. They saw how resurrection and reincarnation warp the natural order, how the world must adjust itself to account for a life returned. The longer the cycle is strained, the more unpredictable the corrections become."_

---

#### **The Mark of the Returned**

_"Some who return are broken, carrying only fragments of the person they were. Others remain unchanged, but the world around them is not so lucky."_

_"A drought where there should be rain. A child never born. A stranger in the market who looks at you as though they know you when they should not. The world does not tolerate imbalance—it shifts, it takes, it replaces."_

_"I have seen a man rise from the grave and, days later, a village swallowed by the sea. Some would call it coincidence. I know better."_

---

#### **The Dragons’ Warning**

_"The old dragons spoke of a ledger, an unseen tally that governs all things. When life is taken, the scales tip. When life is returned, the world must recalibrate."_

_"Some say the dragons themselves were cast down not by blade or spell, but by this very balance. Too many times did they twist fate to suit their will. In the end, the world could bear no more and shifted to erase them."_

---

_"If I bring them back, will I see the cost? Will I wake to find the rivers run dry, the stars reshuffled, the roads leading to places that never were? Will I know, in my heart, that this was my doing?"_
