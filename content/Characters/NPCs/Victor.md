Victor wasn’t born into crime—he chose it. Raised in a wealthy merchant family in the empire’s outer territories, he had everything: education, privilege, and connections. But business was never enough for him. He found trade dull unless it involved control—control over people, over lives.

After a financial scandal ruined his family name, Victor disappeared into the underworld, re-emerging years later as one of the key figures in Mere’s criminal ecosystem. He aligned himself with the **Black Axes**, a brutal mercenary syndicate, and took control of the **Main Town Square**—the beating heart of Mere’s black market. His trade? Flesh. Whether it’s organs for illegal transplants, slaves for labor or pleasure, or even rare species for collectors, Victor ensures that supply meets demand.

His cruelty is not driven by passion but by cold, transactional efficiency. He doesn’t relish the suffering—he simply sees it as a cost of doing business.
