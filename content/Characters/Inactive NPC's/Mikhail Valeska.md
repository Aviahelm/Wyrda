## Backstory

Mikhail wasn’t born into crime—he chose it. Raised in a wealthy merchant family in the empire’s outer territories, he had everything: education, privilege, and connections. But business was never enough for him. He found trade dull unless it involved control—control over people, over lives.

After a financial scandal ruined his family name, Mikhail disappeared into the underworld, re-emerging years later as one of the key figures in [[Mere]]’s criminal ecosystem. He aligned himself with [[The Black Axes]], a brutal mercenary syndicate, and took control of the **Main Town Square**—the beating heart of Mere’s black market. His trade? Flesh. Whether it’s organs for illegal transplants, slaves for labor or pleasure, or even rare species for collectors, Mikhail ensures that supply meets demand.

His cruelty is not driven by passion but by cold, transactional efficiency. He doesn’t relish the suffering—he simply sees it as a cost of doing business.

## Mere's Influence.

With Daveed’s strength coursing through him, Mikhail felt invincible. It was more than muscle — it was command. It was fire that demanded loyalty, magic that bent the world to his will. In the early days, he believed it was a gift. That Mere needed someone strong, someone who wouldn’t flinch. Someone who could protect what remained after [[The Red Raids]].

He convinced himself that his violence had purpose. That fear was a form of protection. That the pain he inflicted was a small price for the safety he could guarantee. And for a time, the people believed it too. He ended threats before they reached the city walls. He stamped out chaos before it bloomed. But strength like Daveed’s was never meant to be wielded by a single man.

Now, Mikhail burns from the inside out. The power that once felt like a gift lashes out in his sleep, shattering rooms, scarring those too close. He sees enemies where there are none. He trusts no one—not even his own men. The more he tries to control it, the more the strength demands to be unleashed. He hungers for war, for carnage, for something to break that might quiet the fire.

And in that hunger, he’s carved a path of ruin. What began as protection has turned into conquest. What began as mercy has become slaughter. He doesn’t know if it’s his paranoia or Daveed’s need for domination that drives him—but he doesn’t care anymore. He’s stopped questioning it.

Mikhail once cleared the wilds so others could live. Now, he clears the living from the land so that no one can challenge him. Daveed’s strength is still inside him—but it no longer answers to Mikhail. It _owns_ him

## His Ending

**Mikhail burned bright—but not from passion. From pressure. From power.**  
He was never the kind to lose control in a fit of rage. His fire was colder than that—measured, efficient, deliberate. That’s what made it so dangerous. He wasn’t cruel because he enjoyed it. He was cruel because it worked.

When the group stood at his stronghold, they knew this wouldn’t be like [[Veronica]]. There was no plea to hear. No memory of kindness to cling to. Deep down, they understood: **Mikhail wasn’t someone they could forgive. He was someone they had to stop.**

The fight was inevitable.  
He sat on the golden throne at the heart of Mere’s hollow empire, dressed in power like armor, daring them to challenge what he’d built. He still believed he was the last line of defense. That the city needed him, even if it hated him. That fear kept people alive.

[[Tarvo]] didn’t hesitate. One arrow.  
Straight through Mikhail’s chest.  
The force of it drove him back, pinned him to the seat he’d refused to abandon. He died the way he lived: gripping power, refusing to let go.

But even in death, he left a mark.  
The curse he’d carried—the one gifted by Daveed, the one that turned fear into fuel—didn’t die with him. [[Kerrick]] stepped forward. Not to redeem Mikhail, but to bear what he couldn’t.
To keep others safe from what was still too dangerous to leave unbound.

**He hadn't just lost his way. He’d let the fire hollow him out until there was nothing left but the monster.**  
And by the time they faced him, **there was no man left to save.**
